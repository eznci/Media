Secure Wi-Fi Connection

Securing your Wi-Fi connection is essential to prevent unauthorized access and protect your network from potential security threats.

Strong Wi-Fi Password: Set a strong and unique password for your Wi-Fi network. Avoid using easily guessable information like your name or address. Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

Privacy Settings

Protecting your privacy online is crucial, especially on social media platforms and other online accounts where personal information is often shared.

Review Default Settings: When creating a new account, review the default privacy settings. Social media platforms often have default settings that may share more information than you're comfortable with. Customize Privacy Settings: Take advantage of customization options. Most platforms allow you to customize who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. Set different privacy levels for different types of content.

Safe Online Shopping

Shop on Secure Websites: Only make online purchases from reputable and secure websites. Look for "https://" in the URL, indicating a secure connection. Avoid making purchases from unfamiliar or suspicious websites. Use Trusted Payment Methods: Opt for secure payment methods, such as credit cards or reputable online payment services. These methods often provide additional protection against fraud. Be cautious when using less secure payment options.

Strong Passwords

Creating strong passwords is crucial for protecting your online accounts from unauthorized access.

Length Matters: Use passwords that are at least 12 characters long. Longer passwords are generally more secure. Mix Character Types: Include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, %). Avoid Personal Information: Avoid using easily guessable information, such as your name, birthdate, or common words. Steer clear of using easily accessible information from your social media profiles.
